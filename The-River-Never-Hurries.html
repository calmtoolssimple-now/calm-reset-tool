The River Never Hurries There is a river not far from where I walk. In spring, it rushes with the melting snow. In summer, it glides quietly beneath the sunlight. In autumn, it carries fallen leaves toward places unknown. And in winter, it rests beneath the ice. Yet through every season, the river remains true to itself. It never hurries. It never worries that it is behind. It never compares its journey to another river. It simply follows its path. For many years, I lived differently. Like so many women, I believed there was always something more to do. Another responsibility. Another deadline. Another person who needed my help. Even during moments of rest, my mind was often racing ahead to the next task. I mistook movement for progress. Busyness for purpose. Exhaustion for accomplishment. Then one day, I began to notice the river. No matter how fast or slow it moved, it always arrived exactly where it was meant to go. The river taught me something I had forgotten. Life unfolds in its own time. The flowers bloom when they are ready. The trees grow one ring at a time. The sun rises without effort. And the river never rushes toward the sea. Perhaps we are not meant to hurry either. Perhaps some chapters require patience. Perhaps some dreams need time to grow. Perhaps healing cannot be forced. Today, if you feel behind, take a moment to remember the river. Place your hand upon your heart. Take a slow breath. Let your shoulders soften. You do not have to arrive all at once. You do not have to know every step ahead. Trust your path. Trust your timing. The river never hurries, yet it always reaches the ocean.
